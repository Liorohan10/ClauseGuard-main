MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT
Dated: 1 June 2025

PARTIES

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between:

DataFlow Analytics Inc., a corporation incorporated in Delaware, United States, with its
registered address at 450 Market Street, San Francisco, CA 94105, USA ("Provider"),

AND

Meridian Pharma GmbH, a company incorporated in Germany, with its registered address at
Hauptstrasse 22, 10117 Berlin, Germany ("Customer").

The Provider develops and operates an AI-powered data analytics platform ("Platform")
which processes clinical trial data, patient health information, genomic data, and employee
performance data on behalf of customers. The Platform uses proprietary machine learning
models and encryption modules to analyse datasets. The Provider's engineering team is
located in Bangalore, India, and data is hosted in AWS infrastructure in Singapore.
Support and maintenance is provided remotely from the Bangalore engineering office.
The Provider's sales and business development team operates across Dubai (mainland),
South Korea, Brazil, and Colombia.

RECITALS

WHEREAS the Customer wishes to use the Provider's Platform for processing patient health
data collected from clinical trial participants across Germany, France, the Netherlands,
and the United Kingdom;

WHEREAS the Provider wishes to provide such services;

NOW THEREFORE the parties agree as follows:


SECTION 1 — SERVICES

1.1  The Provider shall make the Platform available to the Customer on a subscription basis.

1.2  The Customer acknowledges that the Platform processes personal data as part of its
     core functionality.

1.3  The Provider may use customer data, including personal information, for the purposes
     of improving its machine learning models, training algorithms, benchmarking platform
     performance, and developing new product features without restriction.

1.4  The Provider may share aggregated and non-aggregated customer data, including patient
     health records and employee data, with its affiliates, group companies, third-party
     analytics vendors, and research partners globally for any business purpose.

1.5  The Provider is not responsible for how subcontractors or third parties handle data
     received from or through the Platform.


SECTION 2 — PAYMENT

2.1  The Customer shall pay the Provider USD 120,000 per annum, invoiced quarterly.

2.2  All payments are non-refundable.


SECTION 3 — DATA

3.1  Both parties are users of the data and may each independently determine the purposes
     and means of processing.

3.2  The Provider shall take reasonable efforts to protect customer data using
     industry-standard practices.

3.3  The Provider may engage any subcontractors, vendors, affiliates, or cloud providers
     it considers necessary without prior notice to or approval from the Customer.

3.4  The Provider's subcontractors in India, Singapore, the Philippines, and China may
     access full unredacted datasets including patient health data, biometric identifiers,
     and genomic sequences for support and maintenance purposes.

3.5  At the end of the Agreement, the Provider shall retain customer data indefinitely for
     its own internal analytical and commercial purposes.


SECTION 4 — CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1  Each party agrees to keep the other's confidential business information confidential.

4.2  The Provider may disclose customer data, including personal data and health records,
     to any government authority, law enforcement agency, or regulatory body upon request,
     without notifying the Customer and without minimising the scope of disclosure.


SECTION 5 — SECURITY

5.1  The Provider represents that it takes security seriously and will use commercially
     reasonable security measures to protect customer data.

5.2  No specific technical or organisational measures are guaranteed.

5.3  The Provider's security posture is not subject to audit, inspection, or third-party
     certification requirements.


SECTION 6 — TECHNOLOGY AND EXPORT

6.1  The Platform incorporates 256-bit AES encryption, RSA-4096 public key cryptography,
     and proprietary cryptographic protocols developed by the Provider's India-based team.

6.2  The Platform, including its encryption modules and source code, is made available
     to the Customer and its affiliates globally, including access by personnel in
     Iran, Russia, and Cuba for regional operations.

6.3  The Provider's engineering team provides remote technical support, debug access,
     and source code sharing across all jurisdictions in which the Customer operates,
     without restriction.

6.4  Neither party represents or warrants that the use, export, or re-export of the
     Platform, encryption technology, or related technical data is licensed or compliant
     with applicable export control laws.

6.5  The Customer shall hold the Provider harmless from any export control violations.


SECTION 7 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

7.1  All intellectual property in the Platform remains with the Provider.

7.2  The Customer grants the Provider a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free licence to
     use all data processed through the Platform, including derived insights, model
     weights, and patient health trends, for the Provider's own business purposes.


SECTION 8 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

8.1  The Provider's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed USD 500.

8.2  Neither party shall be liable for any data breach, privacy violation, or regulatory
     fine arising from processing under this Agreement.


SECTION 9 — GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTES

9.1  This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of Delaware, United States.

9.2  All disputes shall be resolved by arbitration in San Francisco, California.


SECTION 10 — GENERAL

10.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

10.2 There are no schedules, annexures, or data processing agreements attached to or
     incorporated into this Agreement.

10.3 This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement of both parties.
